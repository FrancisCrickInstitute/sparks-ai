SPARKS: A Biologically Inspired Neural Attention Model for the Analysis of Sequential Spiking Patterns

Nicolas Skatchkovsky1, Sadra Sadeh1, 2, *, Florencia Iacaruso1, *,
1The Francis Crick Institute, 2Imperial College London
Cosyne 2024
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Advances in experimental neuroscience are increasing the capacity to record neuronal activity with higher spatial and temporal resolutions. Predominantly due to its very high dimensionality, interpreting this data in relation to relevant parameters is challenging. A promising approach to address this problem is to focus on meaningful low-dimensional representations of neural data. Recent advances in machine learning (ML) have provided valuable tools to extract such representations. However, existing techniques still often overlook the sequential structure of neural activity, which is crucial for information processing in the brain. To address this limitation, we propose a novel self-attention mechanism that sequentially extracts information from the precise timing of single spikes by incorporating the biological principle of Hebbian learning. The approach involves training a variational autoencoder encompassing the proposed rule using an information-theoretic criterion inspired by predictive coding to enforce temporal coherence in the latent representations. The resulting model is a Sequential Predictive Autoencoder for the Representation of spiKing Signals (SPARKS). It offers a versatile approach, supporting both unsupervised but also supervised learning using e.g. behavioural data to guide the production of relevant latent representations. The embeddings produced are directly interpretable and highly robust across animals and sessions with only tens of neurons recorded when training the model via supervised learning. When training via unsupervised learning, they allow to freely explore the different behavioural or cognitive parameters encoded in the neural data. Finally, the model offers state-of-the-art prediction capabilities on diverse datasets recorded both via electrophysiology and calcium imaging. Overall, combining ML models with biologically inspired mechanisms, SPARKS provides a promising solution for extracting meaningful information from neural data.
